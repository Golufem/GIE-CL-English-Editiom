Global Warming vs. Climate Change

Global Warming refers to the long-term warming of the planet, characterized by a general increase in average temperatures of the Earth. It is caused by enhanced Greenhouse effect.

Climate encompasses global warming, but refers to along time shift or broader range of climate changes that are happening to our planet. These include rising sea levels; shrinking mountain glaciers; accelerating ice melt in Greenland, Antarctica, Arctic; Shifts in flower/plant blooming times. Etc.

GreenHouse Effect

The Sun is the primary source of energy to the Earth. The energy from the sun transmits to earth through the atmosphere as Solar Radiation.

It is not all solar radiation emitted from the sun that reaches earthâ€™s surface. Some are absorbed, reflected or scattered in the atmosphere by air molecules, clouds, dust, etc. This is called solar radiation diffusion. Radiations that is not reflected by the atmosphere reaches the earth's surface and are absorbed by it and warms the earth.

As the earth warms up, the surface starts to emits infrared radiation back into the atmosphere which is absorbed by the Greenhouse Gases (GHG) in the atmosphere.
